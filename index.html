Independence Day Event

Punjab Engineering College (Deemed to be University) celebrated 75th Independence Day with huge Pomp and Joy. And on this great day, PEC Robotics Society added to the flamboyance of this event with their technical display and caused a whim of excitement in audiences. The Robotics team made an eye catching wireless Bluetooth controlled robot through which they served momento to the chief guest and distributed prizes amongst winners. It was a wonderful display of technology. But this wasn't enough, Robotics society also had an amazing drone prepared for flag hoisting ceremony. It was just like cherry on the cake.

  • The Robot was made from an iron chassis built by the students themselves and Arduino was used as the microcontroller in it.
  • Robotics society also had an amazing drone prepared for flag hoisting ceremony.
  • The drone was used to shower petals on the people present in the ceremony.

3D Printing and Fusion 360 Workshop

The Robotics Society organized a workshop on Fusion 360. The workshop aimed to introduce students to the world of 3D modeling and printing and how it can be used to design and create complex objects.

  • The workshop also introduced students to the world of 3D printing, including the different types of 3D printer available and the material used in printing process.
  • Students also learned about the importance of print settings and how to troubleshoot common issues in 3D printing.

Git and Github Workshop

The Robotics Society recently organized a workshop on Git and GitHub for college students. The workshop aimed to introduce students to the world of version control and how it can be used to collaborate and manage code efficiently. Git is a powerful version control system that allows users to track changes to their code and collaborate with others effectively. The workshop covered the basics of Git, including how to create repositories, commit changes, and manage branches.

  • The workshop also introduced students to GitHub, a popular web-based Git repository hosting service. Students learned how to use GitHub to collaborate on projects with others, including how to fork and clone repositories, submit pull requests, and merge changes.
  • The workshop was a great success and provided students with a valuable opportunity to learn about the exciting field of version control and collaboration.

LinkedIn and Resume Building Workshop

The Robotics Society recently organized a workshop on LinkedIn and Resume Building for college students. The workshop aimed to provide students with valuable insights on how to build an impressive resume and develop a strong LinkedIn profile. During the workshop, students learned about the essential elements of a great resume, including formatting, content, and structure. They also received tips on how to tailor their resume for specific job applications and how to highlight their skills and experience effectively.

  • Tailor your resume for each job application to highlight your relevant skills and experience.
  • Use bullet points and action verbs to make your resume concise and impactful.
  • Use your LinkedIn profile to showcase your professional brand and network with others in your field.

Techfest (IIT Bombay)

The Punjab Engineering College (PEC) in collaboration with the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay recently conducted a robotics workshop at the PEC campus in Punjab. The aim of the workshop was to provide hands-on experience to students in the field of robotics and to give them an opportunity to learn from experts in the field.

  • The theoretical sessions provided a solid foundation for the students to understand the basics of robotics, while the practical sessions allowed them to apply their knowledge and build robots.
  • The competition gave students an opportunity to showcase their skills and creativity, and it was great to see the level of innovation and design that went into each robot.
  • It provided students with a unique opportunity to learn from experts in the field, and to gain hands-on experience in building and programming robots.

TechnoXian

A total of 3 teams participated in the competetion, out of which two teams participated in the RC craft competetion and the third team participated in drone competetion, robo race and robo competetion.The 2 teams participating in RC craft competetion were able to secure 1st and 3rd positions.

Roborace

Punjab Engineering College (deemed to be a university) celebrated its Pecfest with great excitement. Among the many thrilling events, the Robotics Society of Pec organized a highly impressive event called Roborace. Students from various colleges participated, showcasing their talents with fast and fearless robots. The team that finished the track in the least time was declared the winner.

  • Students were required to build their robots according to the rules specified by the organizing committee.
  • The track was perfectly designed with twists and turns that challenged the contestants.

Robowar

The Robowar event, held in collaboration with IIT Bombay's Techfest, was a thrilling showcase of the capabilities of robots designed and built by students from various colleges. The competition consisted of a series of rounds in which teams of students competed with their robots to advance through the tournament. The rules were set by the organizing committee, specifying guidelines such as weight limits and other technical specifications for the robots. The ultimate winner of the event, who emerged victorious after a series of intense battles, was given the opportunity to participate in the finals of Techfest.

  • The event was a fierce competition between robots built by students from various colleges.
  • The event was held in collaboration with IIT Bombay's Techfest
  • The winner of the event was given the opportunity to participate in the finals of Techfest.
